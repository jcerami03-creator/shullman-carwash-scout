knowledge to operate a successful high-end full service car wash. The current operation does not offer
the service, hospitality and well trained staff that neighboring retailers excel at and local residents

expect.

The location, layout and buildings are conducive to a very successful hi-end car wash. The location is in
a center of retail activity, easily accessible with an attractive building. The car wash needs an operator
like us, along with a rebranding and renovation to deliver the high-end retail experience local residents
would appreciate.

We plan to offer full service car washing and detailing delivered by a trained, friendly staff. The
renovation would include a build-out of the lobby, construction of canopies to cover vehicles and
associates, new signage, paint, landscaping and car wash equipment.

The Plan

Phase one of the rehabilitation will occur immediately upon closing on the acquisition of the
Property and shall include a cleanup of the cosmetic issues, landscaping, hardscape repair and
upgrading of the equipment for the conversion to an express exterior car wash with ancillary
detailing services. The building and signage, while neglected, needs only cleaning, minimal
repair, and paint to significantly improve the look and feel. The architecture of the building is
iconic of a 1950s modern style known as “Googie Architecture.” Interestingly this is a bit of a
misnomer in that the architect who designed the style is not Googie. Googie was the name of a
coffee shop chain which became synonymous with the style. The building has been identified
by the Los Angeles County and Whittier architectural conservancies as historically significant
and they are supporting Shullman and Winkler in bringing back the wash to its original 1950s
vintage look (see the attached articles). One area where the architectural preservation groups
have been particularly helpful is the support to preserve the unusually large street sign which is
visible from some streets up to a half of a mile away. The City of Whittier identified the sign as
non-conforming with current city codes and slated it for removal. The historical societies are
campaigning to designate it as a historical landmark, and when that occurs Shullman and
Winkler plan to renovate the sign to its original look and use. This includes repairing its ability
to rotate, something it has not done for 20 years.

The conversion to a express exterior with full service and detailing options, also known as “flex
service” will be the basis for the first phase of renovation. Should the wash volume increase as
projected, Shuliman and Winkler may add an additional tunnel for hand car washing, but that
would be only after the property is stabilized.

This document is intended as a brief overview for the Acquisition of the Five Points Car

INTRODUCTION

The 5 Points Car Wash located in Whittier, California (hereinafter the “Property”) is an
exceptional acquisition and rehabilitation opportunity for a seasoned car wash operator. ‘The site
is located on an intersection of five streets which has more than 80,000 cars per day of traffic
and, as a result of a historically unique and unusually large pole mounted street sign, superior
visibility (see the attached maps and recent articles are available upon request).

The Property is 2.82 acres comprised of the car wash tunnel and ancillary car wash uses
including a retail center and detail bays, a 1,800 square foot free-standing retail building, three
industrial buildings totaling 14,000 square feet, and a fenced-in surface parking area which is
currently rented. (see the attached site plan)

The Property is in a high density area which includes approximately 30,000 people in one mile,
162,000 people in 3 miles and 437,000 in 5 miles. Average household income is approximately
$65,000, which is good for the pricing points for the services to be offered at the wash.
Additionally, while car washes in the local vicinity exist, there is a lack of competition for the
proposed pricing and service model: the five-dollar, five-minute car wash.

While no recent appraisal has been conducted, the value of comparable for sale retail and
industrial properties in close proximity to the site indicate a conservative land value with
improvements of between $3,600,000 and $4,500,000 in its existing condition. However, all of
the comparable properties for sale used to estimate a value have inferior locations relative to the
Five Points Car Wash when compared on traffic count and proximity to a hard comer.

As a result of the mismanagement of the car wash business and adjacent commercial property,
profits declined, the seller defaulted on their mortgage and day-to-day operations were taken
back by a court appointed receiver. Originally offered for sale at $5.8 million, Mike Shullman
and Stewart Winkler executed a letter of intent to purchase the Property for $3.2 million.
Shullman and Winkler were able to secure a below market price using the current cash flow as a
basis for its valuation. The Property’s acquisition and rehabilitation will likely yield significant
returns for a seasoned operator with experience in the field rehabilitation and rebranding of car
washes and commercial property management. Because of Mike Shullman’s experience
repositioning car washes, coupled with Stewart Winkler’s experience with commercial buildings,
the team buying the Property is unique in the field in that they have a proven successful track
record in doing just that.
